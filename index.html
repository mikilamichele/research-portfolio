Introduction

Emerging adulthood, defined as ages 18 to 29, is crucial for identity development. This research examines how geopolitical, ethnic, and moral identities shape political attitudes and behaviors in emerging adults in the United States. Understanding these interrelationships helps in addressing societal outcomes in a multicultural world.

Infographic: Overview of the five GEM profiles and their key characteristics.

Theoretical Framework

Geopolitical identities encompass civic, national, and global identities. Ethnic identity involves belonging to an ethnic group, while moral identity reflects the importance of being a moral person. This framework explores how these identities interact to influence political attitudes.

Diagram: Interactive visual illustrating the theoretical framework and how different identities influence political attitudes and behaviors.

Methodology

Participants were emerging adults (ages 18-35) from the United States, recruited through mTurk. The survey included measures for ego identity, ethnic identity, moral identity, civic identity, American identity, global identity, narcissism, altruism, and political ideology.
